It is well known: vertical cities — the largest ones, at least — run on solar energy. The direct consequence of this choice: energy distribution is inevitably inequitable. The further down you go into the lower depths of the city, the less light there is, and the less light there is, the less likely the poor are to feed themselves adequately on energy. To live well, you must live high.
